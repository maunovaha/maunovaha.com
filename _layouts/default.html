An epic journey of a man from beginner to pro in game programming.

By Mauno Vähä under introductionmorpg

Hi, I'm Mauno Vähä and I have an interesting journey ahead. But first, let me briefly introduce myself.

About me

I'm a 28 years old, programmer by work/hobby, student, CS:GO player and a sexy beast.

I've been programming for a quite sometime, many years actually. I got my first experiences to programming over 15 years ago when notepad was still a superb text editor and internet explorer 6 "amazing" browser. Hence, at first, I didn't really knew whether I should become a programmer or a graphic designer; I liked them both. It was only later that I grew to like programming more, mostly because of my studies.

That being said, I've also been studying for a while. I first graduated as a media assistant with a focus on web programming/multimedia and later on received bachelor's degree in information processing science with a focus on software development. Yet, my master's degree is still pending, but I have a good chance to graduate during upcoming year (yay!). Thus, it's one of the reasons why this website exists in the first place. But bear with me, we'll talk about that in a moment.

Finally, among studying, I've been working at the IT industry for a while now. I started off by doing a lot of trainee work in companies and simple websites like this to my family and cousins. Later on, I worked as a software designer in the sports technology industry. Today, I am working as a systems specialist in the financial industry. And, if I were to describe my area of expertise: it's web development using Java/Ruby/JavaScript and a like. However, in order to keep this introduction brief, it's easier if you check out my linkedin profile.

But where's the fun? Don't worry, it's not that I haven't done anything else; I still see people outside the internet - just don't ask from my friends about it. But, within context of this website those stories are rather irrelevant as this website is about the epic journey I'm going to travel. Therefore, trust me when I say this: you won't be seeing any blog posts regarding fashion, makeups and whatnot; not from this site.

Now, let's move into the interesting part.

I've also been making (and trying to make) games and other web applications on and off as long as I have been programming.

At first, I used to make games using programs, such as Game Maker and RPG toolkit. Later on I even tried C++/C#/JavaScript/Python and whatnot. I tried it all, but it wasn't good enough; I wasn't good enough. I have realized after years of programming that getting sh*t done is way more important than low level techniques or editors you are actually choosing. The end user, the player of your game nor the user of what-ever-application you are developing is not going to see that. Developers, like me, often gets caught to a cold hard fact that sometimes choosing of "cool technique" seems to be more important than actually releasing the damn thing. It's not about games, I have seen that sometimes people just love the tools more than actual product.. However, the point is that I have started way too many games and applications than I have actually released; it's umbeliable stressing.
